Hotel Westland Seattle

100 S King Street, Seattle, WA 98104
1-206-3091100 more info show less

Hotel Westland will highlight historic character maintained from the building’s 1907 construction, modern guest rooms and amenities, Pioneer Square’s first rooftop bar, and a signature restaurant fueled by an authentic connection to community and Seattle’s deep-rooted history.

  • About
  • Amenities
  • Gallery
  • Map

Location

Hotel Westland is located in the historic Pioneer Square neighborhood of Seattle, an area known for its vibrant blend of classic architecture, urban parks, and exciting activities. Pioneer Square is Seattle’s oldest neighborhood, filled with Romanesque Revival-style buildings, cobblestone streets, and unique boutiques, galleries, and restaurants. Just steps away from Pioneer Park, home to the iconic iron Pergola and totem pole, offering a tranquil space with greenery, and charming street art that celebrates Seattle’s rich history. Hotel Westland places you close to Elliott Bay and the Puget Sound waterfront, just a short way away. Here, you can enjoy scenic views of the water and skyline, especially stunning during sunsets. The waterfront also offers easy access to a variety of recreational activities, from harbor cruises and kayak rentals to picturesque walking and biking trails. Nearby, Waterfront Park and the Seattle Aquarium are family-friendly highlights, offering marine life exhibits and pier views that capture Seattle’s maritime spirit. Adventurous visitors and locals alike will enjoy the area’s wealth of entertainment options, like the Great Wheel, a giant Ferris wheel offering panoramic views of the city, and an array of seafood restaurants that specialize in fresh, locally caught fare. Nearby historic buildings house diverse art galleries and independent shops that invite exploration. You’re also close to Occidental Square, a vibrant public space with outdoor seating, art installations, and frequent community events, such as live music and pop-up markets, which bring a lively atmosphere year-round.
